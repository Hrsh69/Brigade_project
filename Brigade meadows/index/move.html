Move-In / Move-Out Guidelines

1. Application & Documentation

  • Residents (Owners or Tenants) must submit a completed Move-In / Move-Out Application Form with all required fields.
  • A valid Lease/Rental Agreement must be attached for tenants. The tenant names must match those listed in the form.
  • First-time owners are eligible for one-time free Move-In/Move-Out processing. Charges apply thereafter.

2. Tenant Verification & Bachelor Tenant Policy

  • Bachelor tenants must ensure all names are included in the Lease Agreement.
  • Community regulations restrict occupancy to:
    • 1 Bachelor Tenant – 1 BHK
    • 2 Bachelor Tenants – 2 BHK
    • 3 Bachelor Tenants – 3 BHK
  • All bachelor tenants must sign and submit the Bachelor Tenant Format.

3. Financial Clearance

  • All outstanding dues (CAM charges, penalties, etc.) must be cleared prior to the Move-In or Move-Out process.
  • In cases of unpaid dues, tenants must settle them. If needed, owners must assist in resolving payment issues.

4. KYC & Security Compliance

  • KYC documents (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, etc.) must be submitted to the Estate Management (EM) Office.
  • Move-In / Move-Out charges must be paid via bank transfer or UPI, with UTR number or payment screenshot submitted.
  • Copies of all submitted documents must be provided to the security team; originals go to the EM Office.

5. Timing Restrictions

  • Move-In / Move-Out is allowed only between 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on working days.
  • No move activity is allowed on Sundays or Public Holidays.
  • Violating the timing rules may result in fines or denial of entry to moving staff.

6. Vehicle Registration & Parking

  • Tenants must submit valid vehicle ownership documents (RC, Driving License).
  • Parking stickers will be issued only after verification and completion of all formalities.
  • Parking limits per unit apply. Extra vehicles may not be approved.

7. Lift Usage & Restrictions

  • Passenger lifts must not be used for transporting heavy goods, furniture, or appliances during shifting.
  • Damage caused to lifts during unauthorized use will be chargeable to the resident.

8. System Update & Final Approval

  • All resident/tenant details must be updated in the MyGate system for community security and access control.
  • Final approval for move-in/move-out will be given only upon document and payment verification.

9. Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Unauthorized moves on Sundays or holidays will invite penalties.
  • Improper usage of passenger lifts or damaging common areas may lead to repair cost recovery and fines.
  • Unregistered tenants or lack of proper legal documents may result in eviction or legal notice.
